## 2. Competitor Deep Dive

### 2.1 Monash FODMAP App
- **Developer:** Monash University (academic authority)
- **Price:** $9.99 one-time
- **Platform:** iOS + Android
- **Strengths:** Largest food database, clinician-trusted, traffic-light food ratings, barcode scanner, dietitian-verified data, colorblind mode
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Limited recipe count (storage constraints cited by developers)
  - Minimal meal planning (no weekly auto-planning)
  - No reintroduction phase workflow
  - No restaurant guide
  - No cloud sync between devices
  - No community features
  - UI feels clinical/dated
  - No offline-first full content (data-heavy to download)
- **App Store Rating:** ~4.2/5
- **Key Insight:** The gold standard for food data but explicitly NOT a meal planner

### 2.2 FODMAP Friendly App
- **Price:** Free + IAP
- **Strengths:** Certified product database, some recipes
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Recipes link OUT to third-party websites (not usable offline)
  - Ad-heavy (users report intrusive ads)
  - Navigation glitches reported
  - No meal planning whatsoever
  - Reintroduction phase entirely missing
- **App Store Rating:** ~3.4/5
- **User Complaint Themes (App Store/Reddit):** "ads won't close," "clicked recipe and it opened Safari," "app crashes on reintroduction tab"

### 2.3 Cara Care
- **Price:** Free + $7.99/month premium
- **Strengths:** AI-powered symptom pattern analysis, telehealth dietitian integration (2025), food + stress + medication + bowel movement tracking
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Meal planning is weak and generic
  - Heavy focus on symptom tracking — users must self-manage diet portion
  - Expensive for what's provided relative to competitors
  - Overwhelming for newly diagnosed users (too many tracking fields)
- **App Store Rating:** ~4.0/5
- **Key Insight:** Great for symptom management, poor for diet execution — different job-to-be-done

### 2.4 Spoonful
- **Price:** Free + $3.99/month
- **Strengths:** Excellent barcode scanner (700K+ products), grocery scanner, shopping list, community product-sharing, FODMAP phase support (elimination/reintroduction/personalization)
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Not a meal planner — it's a grocery scanner
  - No recipe library
  - No weekly meal structure
  - Community feature not moderated well
- **App Store Rating:** ~4.3/5
- **Key Insight:** Best product scanner, but users still need a meal planner alongside it

### 2.5 FODMAP Snap / SIBO Tracker
- **Price:** Free + IAP
- **Strengths:** Photo scan for food identification, SIBO-specific content
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Limited recipe content
  - Photo scan accuracy inconsistent
  - Very niche (SIBO focus alienates general IBS users)
- **App Store Rating:** ~3.6/5

### 2.6 Fast FODMAP Lookup & Learn
- **Price:** Free
- **Strengths:** Quick reference, good for beginners
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Reference tool only, no planning
  - No meals, no tracking
  - No reintroduction support

### 2.7 Low FODMAP Diet A to Z
- **Price:** Free
- **Strengths:** Large food list
- **Weaknesses:**
  - No meal planning
  - Basic UI
  - No updates/maintenance visible

### 2.8 Casa de Sante App Ecosystem
- **Price:** Varied (some free guides, paid meal plans as PDFs)
- **Strengths:** Quality content, dietitian-created
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Not a native app — web-based content
  - No interactive planning tool

## 4. User Pain Points (Verbatim Themes from Reddit/App Store Research)

1. **"I have to leave the app to see the recipe"** — FODMAP Friendly links out, breaking offline use
2. **"There's no way to plan a full week"** — Monash is a lookup tool, not a planner
3. **"The reintroduction phase is completely missing"** — Monash shows some data but no guided workflow
4. **"Ads are constant and intrusive"** — Free FODMAP Friendly users
5. **"I don't know which restaurant dishes are safe"** — No existing app has a restaurant guide
6. **"I lose my meal plans when I switch phones"** — No cloud sync in Monash
7. **"The app is confusing for someone newly diagnosed"** — Onboarding is poor across all apps
8. **"I can't generate a shopping list from my meal plan"** — No existing app does this well
9. **"There's no guidance on what to do AFTER the elimination phase"** — Reintroduction workflow gap
10. **"It doesn't work offline on the plane"** — Data dependency in most apps

## 6. Technology Landscape

### Recommended Stack (Validated by Research)
- **Mobile:** React Native with Expo — single codebase for iOS + Android, active community, strong SQLite support
- **Local DB:** Expo SQLite (expo-sqlite) — proven for offline-first food apps, handles complex queries efficiently
- **Backend:** Supabase — PostgreSQL-based, real-time, auth included, generous free tier, Row-Level Security for multi-user data
- **Web:** Next.js 15 (App Router) — for marketing site + web dashboard
- **State Management:** Zustand — lightweight, works well with offline-first patterns
- **Sync Pattern:** Outbox pattern (local changes queue → sync when online) + per-row `updated_at` timestamps for conflict resolution

### Key Technical Insight
Expo SQLite in 2025-2026 supports WAL mode, FTS5 full-text search, and async operations — making it production-ready for a 750-food database with complex FODMAP queries.

## 7. Monetization Research

### Industry Benchmarks
- 70%+ of top diet/nutrition apps use freemium
- Diet & Nutrition subscription revenue up 120% YoY
- In-app purchases = ~45% of total diet app revenue
- Only ~3% of apps use paid-only download (but attracts higher-intent users)
- Health apps: 90-day retention ~34% (better than fitness apps at 31%)

### Structural Advantage for FodmapZen
IBS is a lifelong condition. Unlike fitness apps (6-week churn), FODMAP users need guidance for:
- Elimination phase: 8–12 weeks
- Reintroduction phase: 6–12 months
- Maintenance: ongoing

This means structurally high LTV (Lifetime Value) compared to general health apps.
