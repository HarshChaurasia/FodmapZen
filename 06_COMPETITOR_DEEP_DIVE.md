## 4. Key Strategic Conclusions from Live Data

### Conclusion 1: The Market Leader is Stagnating
Fast FODMAP has 21K ratings but hasn't shipped a meaningful update since March 2024. **This is a window.** Users rate it well because nothing better exists yet, not because it's solving the full problem.

### Conclusion 2: Fodmate is Our Mirror — and Our Biggest Risk
Fodmate is free, has no ads, covers recipes, meal planning, and reintroduction. It's nearly our exact vision but appears to be an indie project without dietitian backing, cloud sync, shopping list, or restaurant guide. If we launch before Fodmate grows its review count (currently 259), we can establish brand authority first.

### Conclusion 3: AI Scanning is the Next Battleground (12–18 Months)
4 apps (FODMAPP, FODMAPai, belio, FODMAP Friendly AI Scanner) are betting on camera-based food analysis. None has meaningful traction yet. FodMap Feast should plan to add barcode/AI scanning in Phase 6 to stay relevant. The quality of the food database + AI combination will be the differentiator — not just having a camera.

### Conclusion 4: The Reintroduction Tracker is Genuinely Unserved
Of all 26 apps surveyed, **zero** offer a proper interactive reintroduction tracker with:
- Guided 3-day test protocol
- Reaction logging (day by day)
- Automated verdict
- PDF export for dietitian review
- Safe food list updating from results

This is still a green field — even Fodmate only has a "guide" (informational), not an interactive tool.

### Conclusion 5: No App Has a Restaurant Guide
Of all 26 apps surveyed, **none** offer a restaurant guide with cuisine-specific safe dish recommendations. This remains completely unserved and is mentioned as a top user pain point on Reddit.

### Conclusion 6: Rating Quality > Rating Volume
Low FODMAP A to Z has 4.8⭐ (9K reviews) with zero marketing investment — purely because it does one thing simply and reliably. Our target: 4.7+ stars through genuine quality, not volume of features.
